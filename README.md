# Apgar Score App – UI/UX Design Project

## 📌 Overview
This project consists of the design of a mobile application interface to calculate the **Apgar Score**, a quick test performed on newborns to assess their health immediately after birth.

The goal of this project is to create an intuitive, efficient, and user-friendly interface that supports different types of users in real-world scenarios.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Design a simple and fast interface for calculating the Apgar score
- Support healthcare professionals in high-pressure environments
- Provide educational support for students learning the Apgar scale
- Allow tracking and comparison of newborn assessments over time

---

## 👥 Target Users (Personas)

### 1. Nurse (Quick Assessment)
Needs to calculate the Apgar score quickly right after birth with minimal interaction.

### 2. Doctor (Monitoring & Comparison)
Needs to store, retrieve, and compare Apgar scores (1-minute and 5-minute) to evaluate the newborn’s condition.

### 3. Student (Learning Support)
Needs guidance and explanations to correctly understand and apply the Apgar criteria.

---

## 🧩 Main Features

- ✅ Quick Apgar score calculation
- ✅ Automatic result interpretation
- ✅ Save 1-minute and 5-minute assessments
- ✅ Compare results over time
- ✅ Patient (newborn) record management
- ✅ Educational mode with explanations for each criterion

---

## 📱 Core Functionality

The application allows users to evaluate the following 5 criteria:

- Skin Color
- Heart Rate
- Reflex Irritability
- Muscle Tone
- Breathing Effort

Each criterion is scored from **0 to 2**, with a total score ranging from **0 to 10**.

### Result Interpretation:
- **0 – 3** → Critical condition  
- **4 – 6** → Requires medical attention  
- **7 – 10** → Normal condition  

---

## 🧪 User Scenarios

### 🩺 Scenario 1 – Nurse
Quickly performs a 1-minute Apgar assessment immediately after birth and saves the result.

### 👨‍⚕️ Scenario 2 – Doctor
Accesses a newborn’s record, performs the 5-minute assessment, and compares it with the previous result.

### 🎓 Scenario 3 – Student
Uses the app to learn about Apgar criteria with explanations and examples before completing an assessment.
